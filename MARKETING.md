## 2. Ideal Customer Profile & Personas

### ICP — Consulting
Growth-stage companies ($1M–$50M ARR) with a marketing or operational problem they can feel but can't diagnose — typically a founder or VP-level operator who has tried to solve it internally and hit a ceiling.

**Persona A — The Scaling Founder**
- Bootstrapped or seed-funded; 10–50 employees; grew by product quality and word-of-mouth but hit an acquisition ceiling
- JTBD: Get to the next revenue tier without hiring a full marketing department
- Pains: Scattered channels, no attribution, unclear CAC, too many vendors
- Desires: One trusted operator who understands both product and growth
- Trigger: Missed a quarter, a competitor is pulling away, or they just closed a round and need to deploy it
- Objection: "We've had consultants before and they just delivered decks"
- Attention: LinkedIn, founder communities (YC, Indie Hackers), podcasts

**Persona B — The Corporate Operator**
- Director/VP at a mid-market company ($10M–$200M); has a team but needs specialized expertise in a specific domain (data, GTM, operations)
- JTBD: De-risk a specific initiative without a 12-month agency retainer
- Pains: Internal politics, slow execution, vendor over-promising
- Desires: Accountability, speed, and someone who has done it before
- Trigger: A board mandate, a new market entry, or an M&A integration
- Attention: LinkedIn, industry conferences, peer referrals

### ICP — Studio (co-founder / partnership)
Founders or domain experts who want to build in gaming, fintech, social, or productivity and need a technical + strategic co-founder model — not just an agency.

### ICP — Investing
Pre-seed/seed founders in consumer software, fintech, and endurance sports who want smart capital with operational leverage, not just a check.

## 5. Brand Voice & Tone

**Personality:** Confident, direct, precise. Not flashy. Earned authority, not claimed authority.
**Voice attributes:** Editorial, specific, outcome-led, no filler, no jargon for its own sake
**Do:** Name the result before the method. Use specific numbers. Write short sentences. Favor nouns over adjectives.
**Don't:** "World-class." "Holistic." "Synergy." "We're passionate about." "End-to-end solutions." Generic superlatives. Decks-speak.
**Sample lines:**
- "We've helped companies acquire their first thousand customers and their next million."
- "Capital deployed where the firm has genuine domain depth — active conviction backed by operational expertise, not passive allocation."
- "Built end-to-end using AI-accelerated development to compress timelines by an order of magnitude."
**Visual identity:** Pure black (#000), pure white (#fff). Three vertical accents: electric blue (consulting), acid lime (studio), warm amber (investing). Playfair Display 900 for display; Inter 800 for headings. Grain texture. No decoration.

## 12. ★ Engagement & LTV Model

**North-star metric:** Consulting — qualified inbound inquiries per month (leading); signed engagements per quarter (lagging)
**Engagement loop:** LinkedIn content → brand recall → referral conversation → inbound contact
**Retention drivers:** Outcomes (clients stay when they see results); the cross-vertical signal (a consulting client becomes a studio partner or investor)
**LTV levers:** Average engagement value × number of engagements per client per year × client lifespan
**Churn risk:** Single-engagement clients who don't convert to retainer; brand visibility dropping if LinkedIn is inactive
**Expansion paths:** Consulting → studio partnership → co-invest; studio product → consulting on the product → invest in the team
**Referral / virality:** Every client should generate 1+ referral; instrument and ask deliberately
